Must-Visit Destinations

Explore Sri Lanka’s iconic landmarks, nature, and hidden gems

Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Sigiriya is one of Sri Lanka’s most iconic landmarks. Rising nearly 200 meters above the surrounding jungle, this ancient rock fortress is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can explore frescoes, mirror walls, and panoramic summit views.

Mirissa & Unawatuna

Located along Sri Lanka’s southern coast, Mirissa and Unawatuna offer pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and laid-back coastal vibes. These destinations are perfect for surfing, whale watching, and relaxation.

Ella & Hill Country

Ella is surrounded by misty mountains, tea plantations, waterfalls, and scenic train routes. Popular attractions include Nine Arch Bridge and Little Adam’s Peak.

Kandy

Kandy is the cultural capital of Sri Lanka and home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic. Nestled among hills, it offers traditional dance, serene lakes, and rich heritage.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is one of Asia’s best wildlife destinations, famous for its high density of leopards. Visitors can experience safaris featuring elephants, birds, and diverse ecosystems.

Galle Fort

Galle Fort is a historic Dutch-era fortress overlooking the Indian Ocean. Cobblestone streets, colonial architecture, cafes, and ocean views make it one of Sri Lanka’s most charming cities.

Sinharaja Rainforest

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sinharaja Rainforest is a biodiversity hotspot with endemic flora and fauna. It’s ideal for eco-tourism, bird watching, and guided nature trails.

Adam’s Peak (Sri Pada)

Adam’s Peak is a sacred pilgrimage site revered by multiple religions. The climb rewards visitors with breathtaking sunrise views and the legendary footprint at the summit.

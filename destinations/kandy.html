Kandy

The Sacred Hill Capital & Cultural Heart of Sri Lanka

Nestled among misty hills in central Sri Lanka, Kandy is a city of immense historical, religious, and cultural significance. As the last royal capital of the island, it remains a living testament to Sri Lanka’s proud heritage and spiritual traditions.

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) is the most revered Buddhist shrine in Sri Lanka. It houses the sacred tooth relic of Lord Buddha, drawing thousands of pilgrims and visitors every day.

Kandy Lake

Built by King Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe in 1807, Kandy Lake adds a serene charm to the city. A walk around the lake offers stunning views of the temple, hills, and colonial architecture.

Esala Perahera Festival

The annual Esala Perahera is one of Asia’s grandest cultural festivals, featuring traditional dancers, drummers, fire performers, and beautifully adorned elephants parading through the streets of Kandy.

Royal Botanical Gardens – Peradeniya

Located just outside the city, the Royal Botanical Gardens of Peradeniya is famous for its extensive collection of orchids, towering palms, and scenic riverbanks.

Sri Lanka’s Beaches

Diverse coasts, golden sands & world-class surf — year-round paradise

Sri Lanka's coastline is astonishingly diverse, offering a perfect beach for every type of traveler. From palm-fringed golden sands to world-class surf breaks and vibrant coral reefs, the island is a year-round beach destination, with the weather simply shifting from coast to coast.

South & West Coasts (Best: November–April)

Bentota & Beruwala: Wide, golden beaches perfect for families and water sports such as jet-skiing and banana boat rides. Calm lagoons make them ideal for beginners.

Hikkaduwa: The original tourist beach, famous for its coral gardens, accessible by glass-bottom boat, along with a vibrant nightlife scene.

Mirissa & Weligama: Mirissa offers a crescent-shaped bay, backpacker vibe, and whale watching tours, while Weligama is Sri Lanka’s premier beginner surf beach with gentle, consistent waves.

Unawatuna & Jungle Beach: A sheltered cove ideal for swimming and dining. A short hike leads to Jungle Beach, a secluded hidden gem.

Tangalle: Long, wilder stretches of coastline with a mix of calm coves like Rekawa Beach and dramatic wave beaches. Perfect for nature lovers seeking peace and beauty.

East Coast (Best: May–September)

Nilaveli & Uppuveli (Trincomalee): Miles of white sand and turquoise waters, often compared to the Maldives. Ideal for swimming, snorkeling at Pigeon Island National Park, and enjoying a relaxed coastal atmosphere.

Arugam Bay: A globally renowned surfing destination featuring Sri Lanka’s most famous point break, “Main Point.” The area has a bohemian surf culture and serves as a gateway to lagoons and bird sanctuaries.

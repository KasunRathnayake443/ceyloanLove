Discover Sri Lanka

The Pearl of the Indian Ocean – Where Ancient Culture Meets Natural Paradise

Explore Now

Rich Cultural Heritage

Ancient Kingdoms

Sri Lanka boasts over 2,500 years of recorded history, with ancient cities like Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa showcasing magnificent ruins of bygone civilizations. Marvel at towering dagobas, intricate stone carvings, and royal palaces that tell stories of powerful kings and queens.

Buddhist Heritage

Home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth in Kandy and the ancient Bodhi Tree, Sri Lanka is one of the oldest Buddhist nations. Experience colorful festivals like Esala Perahera, witness daily rituals, and find peace in serene mountain temples.

Traditional Arts

From vibrant Kandyan dances with rhythmic drumming to intricate handicrafts like mask carving and batik, Sri Lankan culture is alive with artistic expression. Experience traditional music, devil dancing rituals, and contemporary fusion performances.

Culinary Delights

Savor aromatic rice and curry, hoppers for breakfast, kottu roti street food, and world-famous Ceylon tea. Sri Lankan cuisine blends spices, coconut, and fresh seafood into unforgettable flavors influenced by South Indian, Dutch, and Portuguese traditions.

Must-Visit Destinations

Kandy

Kandy is the cultural capital of Sri Lanka and home to the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic. Nestled among hills, it offers traditional dance, serene lakes, and rich heritage.

Elephant Orphanage (Pinnawala)

Pinnawala is a small, rural village in Sri Lanka's Sabaragamuwa Province, most famous for being the home of the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage, established in 1975.

East Coast (Trincomalee)

Trincomalee, located on Sri Lanka’s east coast, is renowned for its tranquil beaches, rich cultural heritage, and one of the finest natural harbors in the world. It offers a perfect blend of relaxation, spirituality, and marine adventure.

West Coast (Kalpitiya)

Kalpitiya, located on Sri Lanka’s northwestern coast, is a hidden gem known for its pristine beaches, lagoons, and thrilling water sports. Unlike crowded tourist hotspots, Kalpitiya offers a peaceful and eco-friendly coastal experience.

Madu River Boat Safari

The Madu River Boat Safari, located near Balapitiya on Sri Lanka’s southwest coast, offers a peaceful and immersive journey through mangrove forests, islands, and diverse wildlife. It is one of the country’s most popular eco-tourism experiences.

Ambalangoda

Ambalangoda is the cultural heart of Sri Lanka’s traditional mask making, famous for vibrant wooden masks used in rituals, storytelling, and folk performances. A unique blend of history, craftsmanship, and spiritual art.

Beaches of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka offers some of the world’s finest beaches — from calm golden bays to world-class surf breaks and coral reefs. A true year-round beach paradise.

Nuwara Eliya

Discover Sri Lanka’s refreshing highlands, endless tea plantations, colonial charm, and the cool climate of “Little England.”

Anuradhapura

Explore Sri Lanka’s sacred first capital, ancient stupas, living Buddhist heritage, and 2,500+ years of remarkable history.
